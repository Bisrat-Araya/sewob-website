OUR FOCUS

WICKEDNESS

Wicked problems are complex, contemporary problems that do not have a conclusive solution and require multidisciplinary thinking. They are intrinsically characterized by uncertainty and involve several stakeholders resulting in a challenge to achieve consensus.

STAKEHOLDERS

Achieving consensus among stakeholders is vital in addressing wicked problems. It ensures the sustainability and adoption of the designed intervention in the long run. The challenge lies in addressing the various values and interests of the numerous stakeholders affected by these problems.

SPATIAL

The majority of data that can be used to make well-informed decisions are location bound. Towards this end, spatial awareness bears significance in understanding where and how wicked problems occur. The clues to understand these complexiities can be unearthed through geospatial insights.

UNCERTAINTY

Uncertainty is a trait of a wicked problem that arises from a poor understanding and gap in knowledge regarding current facts and desired future scenarios. It fogs the path to sustainable solutions for such problems.

OUR APPROACH

Problem Identification

“Conflict of interest” is often a cause of what makes a problem wicked. The key is to define the problem and set pragmatic objectives.

Structuring

Visualizing and analyzing systems through conceptual diagrams and various tools at our disposal

Stakeholder Analysis

Identify every party’s interests and needs, and map relationships between different actors

Geospatial Analysis

Utilizing Remote sensing, GIS, as well as other Geospatial and Data Science technologies, to gain valuable insights

Ideation

Finding ways to bridge geospatial information and stakeholders’ needs and increase awareness among all the parties

Proposal

Designing mutually beneficial interventions to tackle wicked problems and achieve wider consensus
